Vision

Our work seeks to facilitate everyday disruptions of oppressive structures through compassionate action and interpersonal connection.

Everyday Disruptions emphasizes the importance of creating small, applicable disruptions in the way things are done as a tool for practicing or actualizing social change.

As opposed to stagnated empathy, compassionate action implies that an audience has been en-couraged to a place of motivated action through an experience with TRC.

Directing Debut Panel

Association for Theatre in Highter Education

In August 2021, Kristina and Jacob will present a workshop titled "Distance and Connection in Theatre of Radical Compassion" for the Directing Debut Panel at ATHE's 2021 Conference. This workshop uses methodologies from TRC to consider how directors can use distance to facilitate, rather than inhibit, connection within their productions.

Everybody

at Arizona State University

In October 2021, ASU will produce Everybody by Branden Jacobs-Jenkins, directed by Kristina. Jacob will fill the role of "Rehearsal Facilitator" on the show. Together, we will test and put into practice many aspects of our TRC model in this production, including how we organize the production, conduct the rehearsal process, and approach audience engagement. Jacob's work specifically will focus on how we can view the rehearsal process as a space (1) to practice on the small scale the social change we want to see on the big scale and (2) to grow relationships that uplift us and empower us to act in our everyday lives.
